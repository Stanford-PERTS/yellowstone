Data Submission Instructions

Thank you again for agreeing to coordinate this program at your school! Below are instructions for the data submission process. If your schoolâ€™s data will be provided by someone other than yourself, please connect your PERTS Coordinator with the correct person.

Important Note

Please DO NOT send any student data via email! In order to provide the highest level of security for confidential student data, all data must be uploaded to a secure website. The link for uploading data is provided below.

General Instructions

  1. Formatting your Data: Our preference is to receive data as a single csv file. If you would like a sample template, please let your PERTS coordinator know.
  2. Uploading your data:
    1. Create a Box.com accout: You will be uploading your data to a secure folder on Box.com. If you do not have a Box account already, you will need to create one. It is free and simple to create a personal account. You will only need to provide your email address, a password, and a phone number.
    2. Upload your file: When you are ready to submit your data, click the following link and select “Upload”.
      High School Transition Program Data Folder
  3. Notify your PERTS coordinator once your data has been uploaded: Please email your coordinator to let them know once you have successfully uploaded your data.
  4. When to submit data: Please provide student data as soon as it becomes available at the end of the semester.

Provide Data For Which Students

  • Ideally: We would like academic records for all students in your school. This will allow us to compare students in the program to those that were not so that we can perform a more powerful analysis.
  • Minimally: We will need academic records from those students who participated in the program. We can provide a list of these students if necessary.

What Data To Provide

At the end of the semester, please provide the following data:

  • Demographic data including:
    • First and last name
    • Birth date
    • Free or reduced lunch status (if available)
    • Special education status
    • English language learner status
  • Performance data from Academic year 2013-2014, Fall 2014, and Spring 2015 (when it becomes available):
    • Class grades by semester (for all classes taken by each student)
    • Standardized exam results (if available)
  • Absences (either by class or total)
  • Tardies (either by class or total)
  • Suspensions or other disciplinary actions (either in-school or off-site)
  • Other (for example, citizenship or conduct scores)0

Frequently Asked Questions

How long will the data collection process take?

The process of compiling and submitting your students' data typically takes about 1 to 1.5 hours. If it seems as though it will take longer, please speak with your PERTS coordinator to see if we can suggest ways to streamline the process.

Is it okay to provide separate files for grades and other data?

Yes, this is fine.

I won't be the one providing you with data. Is that okay?

Yes, this is fine. Please provide us with the contact information of the person who will provide data prior to the beginning of the study. This will allow us to make sure they are clear on the timeline and on what they will need to provide.

What kind of suspension and/or discipline data should I provide?

Whatever your school's system is for suspensions (for example, in-school versus at-home) and other disciplinary actions (for example, detensions) will be fine. You can provide a description of abbreviations used if necessary.
