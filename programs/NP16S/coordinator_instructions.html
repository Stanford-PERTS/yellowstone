Coordinator Instructions

Thank you for agreeing to coordinate the High School Transition Program program at your school! Your role is particularly important. You will be our main point of contact with your school and will be responsible for helping teachers schedule their classes, arranging computer lab access, and making sure that the program runs smoothly.

Below is the contact information for the study coordinator and a checklist of what will be required to oversee this project.

Scheduling

Sessions 1 and 2 will each take about 45 miuntes. The program will open on January 12th and close on March 27th. All three sessions must be completed within that window.

  • Session 1 should be completed in January or early February.
  • Session 2 should be completed about 1 month after Session 1.

**Note - Each session should take place at a time when students can pay full attention. Based on our previous data, it is best not to schedule sessions during the following times:

  • The period before or after lunch
  • The last period of the day
  • On a Friday
  • In the week leading up to a break
  • During the week of standardized testing

Coordinator Checklist

To insure smooth program implementation, please follow these steps.

  1. Email your PERTS coordinator a signed copy of the School Letter of Agreement.
  2. Notify parents about the study at least 2 weeks before students are scheduled to participate by sending home the parent information letter provided under the Documents tab. Parents do not need to sign these, but can choose to opt their child out if they wish.
  3. Work with teachers to schedule when their students will complete both sessions and help coordinate computer lab access.
  4. Login at perts.net to add and schedule participating classrooms under the Schedule tab.
  5. For each participating classroom, add the classroom roster by clicking on the class under the Class Name column and then clicking onAdd Student(s). You can enter the names individually, or you can copy and paste from a pre-existing roster.
  6. Send the High School Transition Program Session Instructionsto participating teachers and provide support if needed. This document is located on the Documents page.
  7. Prepare computer labs. Test the students’ survey link on a computer in your school’s computer lab:
    1. Sign into a computer using the same type of login credentials a student would use (i.e., don’t log in as an administrator).
    2. Important: Our program supports Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Internet Explorer versions 8 and up. Please note that Windows XP has known issues with audio. If audio doesn't work in one browser, try another one.
    3. Go to perts.me to check that you are able to access the student login page.
    4. If you are, go to perts.net/help and click all the way through to the end to make sure you do not encounter any issues. If you do, please call your coordinator at the above number.
  8. Provide students with headphones during the program so they can listen along to the audio. If you are NOT able provide headphones, please let your PERTS coordinator know as soon as possible.
  9. Collect the Student Make-up List from teachers after each session. Update students’ participation status under the Participation Code column in the Classroom Roster tab.
