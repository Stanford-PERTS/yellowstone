Coordinator Instructions

Thank you for agreeing to coordinate the Middle School Transition program at your school! Your role is particularly important. You will be our main point of contact with your school and will be responsible for helping teachers schedule their classes, arranging computer lab access, and making sure that the program runs smoothly.

Below is the contact information for the study coordinator and a checklist of what will be required to oversee this project.

Scheduling

Sessions 1 and 2 will take about 45 miuntes each. Session 3 will take about 15 minutes. The program will open on September 15th and close on November 15th. All three sessions must be completed within that window.

  • Session 1 should be scheduled as close to September 15th as possible.
  • Session 2 should be scheduled 1-2 weeks after session 1.
  • Session 3 should be scheduled 3-4 weeks after session 2.

Coordinator Checklist

To insure smooth program implementation, please follow these steps.

  1. Email your PERTS coordinator a signed copy of the School Letter of Agreement.
  2. Notify parents about the study by sending home the parent information letter provided under the Documents tab. Parents do not need to sign these, but can choose to opt their child out if they wish.
  3. Work with teachers to schedule when their students will complete all three sessions and help coordinate computer lab access. Each session should take place at a time when students can pay full attention. Our previous research suggests that it is best not to schedule sessions in the week before a break or on a day when the class schedule is disrupted by standardized testing.
  4. Login at perts.net to add and schedule participating classrooms under the Schedule tab.
  5. For each participating classroom, add the classroom roster by clicking Add Student under the Classroom Roster tab. You can enter the names individually, or you can copy and paste from a pre-existing roster.
  6. Send the Middle School Transition Program Instructions, located under the documents tab, to participating teachers and provide support if needed.
  7. Prepare computer labs. Test the students’ survey link on a computer in your school’s computer lab:
    1. Sign into a computer using the same type of login credentials a student would use (i.e., don’t log in as an administrator).
    2. Important: Our program supports Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Internet Explorer versions 8 and up.
    3. Go to perts.me to check that you are able to access the student login page.
    4. If you are, go to perts.net/test and click all the way through to the end to make sure you do not encounter any issues. If you do, please call your coordinator at the above number.
  8. Provide students with headphones during the program so they can listen along to the audio. If you are NOT able provide headphones, please let your PERTS coordinator know as soon as possible.
  9. Collect the Student Make-up List from teachers after each session. Update students’ participation status under the Participation Code column in the Classroom Roster tab.
  10. Provide student transcript data. At the end of the semester you will upload the academic records of your stu- dents to a secure website. We will use these records to analyze the effects of the program. See Data Submission Instructions in the documents tab for more information.
